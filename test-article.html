Merkel: Paris climate agreement is 'irreversible and non-negotiable'

Friederike Heine

Jun 29, 2017

Berlin (dpa) - The Paris climate agreement is "irreversible and non-negotiable," Chancellor Angela Merkel told the German Bundestag on Thursday, eight days ahead of the Group of 20 summit in Hamburg.

The European Union's remaining 27 members have expressed their "unequivocal" commitment to the accord despite the United States' decision to leave, Merkel told the Bundestag.

US President Donald Trump announced earlier this month that his country would cease participation in the 2015 agreement to halt climate change.

In an implicit reference to Trump, Merkel said that those "who believe the problems of this world can be solved though isolationism and protectionism are sorely mistaken - only together will we manage to find the right answers to the central questions of our time."

Addressing the Bundestag after the chancellor, Thomas Oppermann - parliamentary group leader for the centre-left Social Democrats - said that Merkel should use the summit to create a "19-to-1 alliance" excluding the US after its decision to abandon the agreement.

After her speech in parliament, Merkel met British Prime Minister Theresa May for a 30-minute meeting in preparation for the summit.

Later on Thursday, she was due to meet Spain's Mariano Rajoy, France's Emmanuel Macron, Italy's Paolo Gentiloni, Dutch Prime Minister Mark Rutte and Norway's Erna Solberg, as well as EU leaders Jean-Claude Juncker and Donald Tusk, also in preparation for the summit.

Merkel is hosting the G20 summit on July 7-8, which will bring together leaders including Trump, Russian President Vladimir Putin and Turkish President Recep Tayyip Erdogan.

Tens of thousands of protesters are expected to descend on the northern German city next week, and authorities are preparing for some demonstrations to turn violent.

Merkel said that protests were "more than legitimate in a democracy," but said she hopes they will remain peaceful.
